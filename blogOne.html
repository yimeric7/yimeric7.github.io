The Design of Everyday Things: Chapter One

#WordJournal

Word

Affordances.

Explanation

In Chapter 1 of "The Design of Everyday Things," Don Norman introduces the concept of "affordances," referring to the perceived and actual properties of an object that determine how it can be used. For instance, a chair affords sitting, and a knob affords turning.

Importance to Career

As an aspiring entrepreneur, understanding "affordances" is critical. It's not just about creating a product or service; it's about ensuring that the intended users can instinctively understand its purpose and use it efficiently. If users can immediately grasp what an object is for and how to use it based on its design, it reduces the barriers to adoption. In a highly competitive marketplace, simplifying user interactions can be a significant advantage. By aligning product design with user expectations through clear affordances, I can increase the likelihood of product success and user satisfaction.
